A Personal Welcome from Our Head

Ms C Phelps

Head of School

Dear Future More House Family,

I am delighted to extend a heartfelt welcome to More House School. This is a truly special place where tradition and innovation unite to create an exceptional educational experience.

At More House School, our mission is to cultivate an environment where pupils can excel academically, exhibit kindness in all that they do, develop essential life skills, and embrace their unique strengths and talents.

We are committed to independent education, grounded in the belief that every child has unique gifts and aspirations that deserve to be nurtured and celebrated.

Within our historic walls, we create a supportive and forward-thinking atmosphere that encourages each pupil's growth. Our curriculum is thoughtfully designed to inspire curiosity, promote critical thinking, and instill a global perspective. By balancing academic challenges with a variety of co-curricular activities, we nurture girls to become compassionate, confident, and responsible citizens, equipped with the resilience and purpose needed to face the complexities of the modern world.

Our central London location offers a unique and stimulating setting, where pupils are surrounded by endless opportunities for exploration and inspiration. From world-class cultural institutions to the vibrant life of the city, our girls thrive in an environment that fosters a lifelong love of learning.

What truly sets More House apart is our deeply caring and inclusive community, where every pupil feels valued and supported. Our dedicated staff are committed to nurturing the academic, social, and emotional wellbeing of each girl, helping them reach their full potential in every aspect of life.

I look forward to meeting with you and welcoming you into the More House family.

Ms C Phelps
Head

London as Our Extended Classroom

Where the capital becomes your laboratory, gallery, and stage

City Curriculum Days

Learning Beyond Four Walls

Every Half Term - Off Timetable Learning

Our City Curriculum Days take pupils from Years 5-9 off regular timetable to explore the capital's cultural treasures, making real-world connections with classroom learning across all subjects. In the heart of one of the world's greatest cities, we transform museums, galleries, parks and historic sites into extensions of our classrooms.

Science: Hyde Park Fieldwork

Using London's largest royal park as our natural laboratory for ecological studies, biodiversity surveys, and environmental science investigations.

History: Living Museums

Regular partnerships with the Natural History Museum, Science Museum, and V&A bring historical periods to life through immersive exhibitions and workshops.

Business: City Explorations

Visits to the City of London for real-world business applications, including economics fieldwork and understanding global financial markets.

Drama: West End Partnerships

Theatre trips, backstage tours, and workshops with professional companies, plus performances on actual West End stages for school productions.

Art: Gallery Collaborations

Regular visits to world-class galleries including the Tate, National Gallery, and contemporary spaces, with sketch workshops and curatorial projects.

Languages: Cultural Immersion

Experiencing authentic cultural venues, from embassy events to international festivals, bringing modern languages to life in cosmopolitan London.
